Introduction

SigmaSpectra is a computer program that allows for ground motions to be selected from a library of motions to fit both a target median response spectrum and standard deviation. A detailed description of the methodology is presented here. If this is your first time using SigmaSpectra please read through the tutorial.

The program was developed by Albert Kottke as part of his Master's and Doctoral research with Professor Ellen Rathje at the University of Texas at Austin.

Disclaimer: There has been little work to validate the results of this scaling method with results obtained through other scaling methods. However, because of the level of automation provided it is easy to generate suites of motions without considering aspect of the ground motions not captured by the response spectrum. This program is not a substitute for engineering judgement.
